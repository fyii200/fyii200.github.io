I am a data scientist with research interests in common eye diseases (particularly myopia and its associated complications). I work with large-scale inaging data and electronic health records from sources such as [SCONe](https://clinical-sciences.ed.ac.uk/ophthalmology/scone/about-scone) and the [UK Biobank](https://www.ukbiobank.ac.uk) to develop computational approaches to personalised risk prediction and investigate important epidemiological questions. I am currently funded by an independent Transition Fellowship at the University of Edinburgh and hold a visiting research position at King’s College London. 

I received my PhD in precision medicine with integrated study in data science from the University of Edinburgh, following my undergraduate training in the Department of Vision Sciences at Glasgow Caledonian University.
